About CEAMLS

Center for Equitable AI & Machine Learning Systems

The Center for Equitable Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Systems (CEAMLS) at Morgan State University is positioned to make a substantial impact on multiple levels. The center serves as a vital hub for advancing equitable AI research, education, and community engagement.

CEAMLS hosts the National Symposium on Equitable AI each year, bringing together technologists, researchers, policymakers, and stakeholders for critical and constructive reflection on artificial intelligence's past, present, and future, with special attention to issues of equity and racial bias.

Conference Theme 2026

"AI for All: Building Inclusive Technologies for a Diverse Society"

The 2026 symposium focuses on the critical need to develop AI systems that serve all communities equitably. We explore how emerging technologies can be designed, deployed, and governed to reduce rather than amplify societal inequities.

Research Focus

Algorithmic bias detection, fairness metrics, and inclusive design methodologies

Industry Applications

Real-world case studies of equitable AI implementation across sectors

Policy & Governance

Regulatory frameworks, ethical guidelines, and accountability mechanisms

Community Engagement

Participatory design, community-based research, and stakeholder involvement
