Call for Papers 2026

The National Symposium on Equitable AI (NSEA) 2026 invites researchers, practitioners, policymakers, and community advocates to submit original research contributions that address the critical challenges of developing fair, inclusive, and equitable artificial intelligence systems.

We welcome submissions that advance our understanding of AI equity through theoretical analysis, empirical research, case studies, policy analysis, and community-engaged research.

Research Topics

We invite submissions on the following topics and related areas

01

Algorithmic Fairness & Bias

  • Bias detection and mitigation techniques
  • Fairness metrics and evaluation frameworks
  • Intersectionality in AI systems
  • Causal inference for fairness
  • Algorithmic auditing methodologies
02

Inclusive AI Design

  • Human-centered AI development
  • Participatory design in AI systems
  • Accessibility in AI applications
  • Cultural sensitivity in AI
  • Universal design principles for AI
03

Data Justice & Representation

  • Data sovereignty and ownership
  • Representative dataset construction
  • Privacy-preserving equitable AI
  • Synthetic data for fairness
  • Community data governance
04

AI Policy & Governance

  • Regulatory frameworks for equitable AI
  • Accountability mechanisms
  • AI impact assessments
  • International AI governance
  • Legal frameworks for AI fairness
05

Domain-Specific Applications

  • Healthcare AI equity
  • Education technology fairness
  • Criminal justice AI systems
  • Financial services fairness
  • Employment and hiring AI
06

Community & Social Impact

  • Community-based participatory AI research
  • AI for social good
  • Digital divide and AI access
  • AI education and literacy
  • Stakeholder engagement in AI development

Submission Guidelines

Choose the submission type that best fits your research

01

Full Papers

  • 8-12 pages (excluding references)
  • Original research contributions
  • Comprehensive evaluation
  • Oral presentation (20 min + Q&A)
  • Publication in proceedings
02

Short Papers

  • 4-6 pages (excluding references)
  • Work-in-progress or position papers
  • Preliminary results
  • Poster presentation
  • Publication in proceedings
03

Extended Abstracts

  • 2-4 pages (excluding references)
  • Novel ideas or perspectives
  • Early-stage research
  • Lightning talk (5 min)
  • Online publication
04

Formatting Requirements

Format Specifications

  • ACM Conference format (double-column)
  • PDF format only
  • Anonymized for double-blind review
  • English language
  • References not counted in page limit

Content Requirements

  • Clear problem statement
  • Related work discussion
  • Methodology description
  • Results and evaluation
  • Ethical considerations section
