Workshop

Agentic Software Development - ACT

Day 2 · Sunday, July 26 · 10:25 AM to 12:00 PM · Workshop Room 208

Workshop participants must bring their own laptop.

Abstract

Recent advances in large language models have transformed software engineering by enabling autonomous coding agents capable of reasoning over project context, modifying source code, executing development tasks, debugging applications, and supporting deployment under human supervision. Despite this progress, computing education continues to emphasize prompt engineering and code generation, providing limited opportunities for students to develop the competencies required to effectively collaborate with autonomous coding agents throughout the software development lifecycle. This gap highlights the need for instructional approaches that prepare learners for emerging software development practices.

This workshop introduces participants to Agentic software development through an experiential, project-based learning framework centered on the end-to-end development and deployment of a modern web application. Organized into progressive laboratory modules, the workshop covers project setup, React application development, iterative interface refinement, version control, web deployment, and agent-assisted debugging. Throughout the workshop, participants learn to direct coding agents, evaluate implementation plans, approve or reject proposed modifications, verify generated artifacts, and maintain responsibility for software quality through a human-in-the-loop development process.

By integrating contemporary development tools with project-based learning, participants build and deploy a fully functional web application while developing practical competencies in agent-collaborative software engineering, deployment workflows, and software validation. The workshop provides a scalable instructional framework for integrating agentic software development into computing and engineering education, equipping students, educators, and researchers with the skills needed to work effectively alongside autonomous coding agents in modern software engineering practice.

Workshop

FARS (FIFA-Automated Reporting System)

Workshop participants must bring their own laptop.

Abstract

FARS (FIFA-Automated Reporting System) is an automated pipeline for AI-generated football match reporting with human-in-the-loop editorial review. It is a Python-based pipeline that retrieves structured football match data from the api-football API, generates natural-language match summaries using large language models, and pushes them to a newsroom system for review and editing by student journalists prior to publication.

FARS addresses the practical challenge of producing timely sports coverage at scale while preserving editorial oversight, situating automated generation within an established human-in-the-loop workflow rather than fully autonomous publishing. We describe the system architecture, the data and generation pipeline, and the editorial integration, and discuss directions for evaluating factual reliability in domain-specific automated journalism.

Anjolie Anthony is a Research Engineer specializing in artificial intelligence and AI-driven tools. Her work focuses on machine learning, large language models (LLMs), and AI visualization, with an emphasis on making complex systems more interpretable, reliable, and practical for real-world applications.

Her current interests include explainable AI, multimodal learning, and building AI systems that support research, decision-making, and public-sector applications.

From Digital Models to Immersive Digital Twins for Civil Engineering Education and Infrastructure Applications

Day 1 · Saturday, July 25 · 3:30 PM to 4:45 PM · Workshop Room 208

Workshop participants must bring their own laptop.

Abstract

This workshop will introduce participants to a practical and scalable process for developing digital twins for civil-engineering education, infrastructure visualization, inspection, resilience assessment, and asset management.

Rather than using a presentation-only format, the session will include a guided live demonstration showing how engineering drawings, photographs, BIM or 3D models, asset information, inspection records, and scenario data can be integrated into an interactive digital environment. Where the venue permits, participants will also have an opportunity to experience the model through virtual reality.

The methods include

  • Data acquisition and preparation using drawings, photographs, GIS, BIM, scanning, and inspection records
  • Development and optimization of a civil-infrastructure 3D model
  • Integration of component information, condition data, maintenance history, and inspection observations
  • Interactive and immersive visualization
  • Application of an engineering scenario, such as flooding, deterioration, access restriction, or maintenance prioritization
  • Participant engagement through guided exercises, discussion, and a short feedback assessment
